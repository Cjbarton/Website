Can I be both a Student & Mother fulltime?

Why Do I Question this?

Recently, the entire province of B.C. has to practice Social Distance due to a World Health Pandemic of a contagious virus known as Covid-19. How does that affect me? Wellâ€¦ I am a student participating in a Foundations in Innovations & Technology Program which was being delivered community-based (not on a regular school campus in a community we reside an hour away from). I am also a mother of 2 children under the age of 5, and expecting a 3rd child in early June.

I was not prepared to relocate back home, and figure out how to be a focused student and give the best care to my children at the same time...

Here are changes that have happened with Social Distancing:

  • our classes had to adjust from in-class face-to-face delivery to online-based.
  • No gathering with anyone you do not live with.
  • No School/Childcare for my kids to attend.
  • No going out to do social activities(playgrounds, recreational sports, visiting friends & family)
  • Only going to shop for what you need (Food, medical prescriptions), getting medical attention if absolutely needed.

It has been a big adjustment. I felt that it would be impossible to balance being both a primary caregiver and a flourishing student. I wanted to give up because I really thought it would be too much for me. However, self-defeat is always a challenge that I face, that I do not want my kids to learn from me. A commitment I made to myself is I would see myself through this program no matter what it takes.
